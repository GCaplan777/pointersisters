The Pointer Sisters

With the recent passing of Bonnie Pointer, I'm compelled to keep celebrating and exploring the rich musical legacy of this iconic sister vocal group. As a child of the 80's, their biggest hits were the soundtrack to my young life. As a young adult, I found their earlier period with Bonnie–their incredible jazz, bebop singing and their amazing covers of America's great composser, Allen Toussaint. As I grow, I love each period from the Pointer Sisters more so. Putting on "Jump" and "Automatic" and "I'm So Excited" today, with a more refined sense of music production blows me away in how the songs were made–but I also retain that joy from my youth in hearing the songs, waching the videos. These ladies are a gift to all of us.

Bio and Founding Members

"The Pointer Sisters are an American R&B singing group from Oakland, California that achieved mainstream success during the 1970s and 1980s. Spanning over four decades, their repertoire has included such diverse genres as pop, disco, jazz, electronic music, bebop, blues, soul, funk, dance, country and rock. The Pointer Sisters have won three Grammy Awards and received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1994. The group had 13 US top 20 hits between 1973 and 1985." from Wikipedia

  • Ruth Pointer (1972–present)
  • Anita Pointer (1969–2015)
  • Bonnie Pointer (1969–1977; died 2020)
  • June Pointer (1969–1977, 1978–2002; died 2006)
